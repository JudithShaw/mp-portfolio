Video and Photography Basics


LIGHTING - Different lighting styles was used in creating the 5 photographs to represent The Circle of Life. For the video project Street Talk the camera was adjusted to adapt to the natural lighting conditions at the time of shooting.

FRAMING - This is a photography basic that is sometimes neglected. The first choice is whether to shoot horizonal or vertical.

The TYPE OF SHOTS is mainly a cinematographic concept but it can also apply to photography. There are 3 basics types of shots: long shots, medium shots and close-up shots. (Hakimi, September 29, 2010)

CHOICE OF TOOLS There was a combination of DSLR and mobile phone camera to capture the images. The last photo was a screenshot from video footage and enhanced in photoshop. All photographs were taken in jpg format.

VIDEO PRODUCTION BASICS There are several things that needs to be taken into consideration when filming and editing video. Dan Shewan’s blog on Word Stream (Shewan, August 15, 2020) gives 15 tips for filming which includes; Pre-production – creating a storyboard, preparing interviews, planning shots. Production – covering the ‘Rule of Thirds’ which enables good composition, lighting, white balance and sound.

The Editing process is where all the planning and production comes together. Any mistakes can sometimes be fixed at this stage. It is important to assemble and store the footage so that the process is made more manageable.
